experience

Teaching Assistant

Sept. 2022 - Present

Embry-Riddle Aeronautical University

As a Teaching Assistant for the Java Object-Oriented Programming (OOP) course at Embry-Riddle Aeronautical University (ERAU), I play a pivotal role in guiding and supporting students' learning experiences. My dedication to their success and passion for Java programming enable me to make a meaningful impact in their educational journey.

Responsibilities:

Leading Engaging Lab Sessions: I conduct interactive lab sessions, guiding students through hands-on exercises to apply Java OOP concepts in practical scenarios. By providing clear instructions and fostering an inclusive learning environment, I empower students to strengthen their programming skills.

Offering Individualized Support: Recognizing that every student has unique learning needs, I offer one-on-one tutoring sessions to provide personalized guidance and assistance. Whether it's clarifying complex concepts or troubleshooting code, I am committed to helping students overcome challenges and build confidence.

Providing Constructive Feedback: I meticulously grade students' assignments and projects, offering detailed feedback . By providing constructive help, I motivate students to continuously enhance their programming abilities.

Holding Informative Office Hours: I hold regular office hours to provide a supportive and accessible environment for students. During these hours, I address questions, offer guidance, and share additional resources to further enrich their understanding of Java and OOP.

Cybersecurity Research Intern

May 2023 - July 2023

National Science Foundation (NSF)

As a Cybersecurity Research Intern at NSF-ERAU, I have been fortunate to contribute to cutting-edge research in the realm of unmanned aerial vehicles (UAVs) by leveraging the power of software-defined radios (SDRs). In this role, my primary objective is to identify and address vulnerabilities in UAV communications.

To achieve this, I have engaged in comprehensive academic literature reviews, analyzing existing research and staying up to date with the latest advancements in UAV security. By assimilating knowledge from a diverse range of sources, I have developed a solid understanding of the key challenges and emerging trends in the field.

Moreover, I have had the privilege of attending informative speaker sessions featuring experts from across the nation. These sessions have expanded my perspective and provided valuable insights into real-world cybersecurity practices, enabling me to enhance my skill set and apply them effectively to my research endeavors.

I have gained hands-on experience in utilizing SDRs to simulate and analyze UAV communication systems. This practical exposure has allowed me to identify potential vulnerabilities, assess their impact, and propose effective countermeasures. Through collaborative efforts, we have successfully executed various projects, each contributing to the broader goal of improving UAV cybersecurity.

Overall, my internship at NSF-ERAU has equipped me with invaluable research and cybersecurity skills. Through a combination of hands-on experimentation, literature reviews, and collaborative projects, I have developed a deep understanding of UAV communications and the associated security challenges. I am confident in my ability to identify vulnerabilities and contribute to the development of robust solutions that safeguard critical UAV systems.
